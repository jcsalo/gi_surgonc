GI Surgical Oncology

14 Esophagectomy Postop Ward

Once stable, patients are transferred to 6T. If a 6T bed is not available, please notify Dr Salo. Historically, over 95% of patients are transferred to 6T after leaving the ICU.

Postoperative stay is typically 5-6 days for younger patients and 7-8 days for older patients.

Patients are discharged to home in 70% of cases. Most of the remainder are discharged to acute rehabilitation.

Discharge Medicines

Patients after esophagectomy typically go home with the following medicines:

  • Proton pump inhibitors (will continue for 2 years)
  • Oxycodone elixir via feeding tube. Current STOP guidelines dictate that patients receive no more than a 7 day supply of opiods at discharage
  • Reglan 10mg po qid (will stop at 6 weeks post-op)
  • Remeron 15mg qhs (will continue for 3 months post-op)
  • Tylenol 1000mg q6 hours as elixir (pediatric form)
  • Gabapentin 300mg as liquid tid x 14 days
  • Metoprolol if started postoperatively (most patients). If not on beta blockers preoperatively, this will be cut in half at the first visit and stopped at the second postoperative visit. It is important to limit the use of medicines via jejunostomy in order to lower the risk of clogging the feeding tube. While liquid metoprolol is available for inpatients, it is not available from outpatient pharmacies.

Esophagectomy - Swallowing {#eso_swallow}

Esophagectomy Ward Diet Orders

Patients who undergo esophagectomy are at risk for swallowing dysfunction due to pharyngeal muscle atrophy and in patients with a cervical anastomosis, from recurrent laryngeal nerve dysfunction and the local effects of surgery.

Once the nasogastric tube has been removed AND there is some certainty that the patient is a low risk for an anastomotic leak, evaluation for swallowing dysfunction is performed prior to the start of oral diet.

Patients who are considered high risk for anastomotic leak (elevated drain amylase OR wound infection in the context of a cervical anastomosis OR leukocytosis) are evaluated with CT esophagram PRIOR to swallowing evaluation.

Modified Barium Swallow MBS is performed after removal of the nasogastic tube. Video fluoroscopy is performed while the patient sips barium of various consistencies. This study will result in one of four outcomes:

Cleared for thin liquids. These patients are started on 1oz of water per hour, along with oral medicines. In those who have good gastric empyting (as evaluated on upper GI), protein shakes are started at 2oz per hour the following day if oral water is well tolerated. Cleared for nectar-thick liquids. These patients are started on oral medicines with nectar-thick water. They are otherwise kept NPO. Fail nectar-thick liquids. These patients are kept NPO and medicines are administered via jejunostomy tube.
